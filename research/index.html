We need to make a novel application of spectral theory to couple the time variation of the chord/arc-profile with the spatial viscosity equation and the construction of comparison functions is more complicated.

Ancient solutions to the curve shortening flow on the two-sphere (with Janelle Louie)

  • Status:
    • Mathematics: Essentially complete, up to dotting the I's and crossing the T's in our calculations.
    • Writing: The outline is mostly written, but needs to be compiled into a cohesive whole.
  • Summary: We prove that the only ancient solutions to the curve shortening flow on the 2-sphere are families of shrinking geodesic circles. An application of the Gauss-Bonnet theorem allows us to show that ancient solutions must have backwards limit an equator. An Alexandrov reflection argument then completes the proof.

A Harnack inequality for evolving hypersurfaces in hyperbolic space (with Xuzhong Chen).

  • Status:
    • Mathematics: The program is defined, we just need to check that it works.
    • Writing: Nothing much yet.
  • Summary: We are attempting to prove a Harnack inequality for hypersurface flows in hyperbolic space, particularly in three dimensions. The technique is inspired by Harnack inequalities for evolving hypersurfaces by Ben Andrews. Mathematische Zeitschrift September 1994, Volume 217, Issue 1, pp 179-197. We can define a support function for a hypersurface and transfer out study to the support function. The complications that arise are in dealing with the curvature terms and terms related to the distance function arising from the hyperbolic metric. We must also uniformly relate the Wulff shape of a hypersurface with the original hypersurface adding further complication to the method.

A distance comparison theorem for networks evolving by curve shortening.

  • Status:
    • Mathematics: I essentially have a comparison theorem. I'm a little unsure about the best formulation of the free-boundary problem. I also don't know what comparison to choose though I have a guiding idea.
    • Writing: Only the basic variational argument is written so far.
  • Summary: The idea here is to investigate whether a distance comparison principle holds for networks as a model for other lower regularity problems. A comparison theorem in the spirit of Huisken has been obtained in Evolution of convex lens-shaped networks under curve shortening flow. The idea here is to obtain stronger control via viscosity methods and comparison principles.
