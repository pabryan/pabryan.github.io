Quizzes

Instructions

There will be an online quiz due each Monday at 11am. The quiz is available on TED and can be taken anytime before the due date. You don't need to complete it in one sitting.

You should read the relevant sections (see Schedule below) of the text in advance of taking the quiz. Just skim through the text, reading the definitions and theorem statements (not necessarily proofs) and skim over a few of the examples. Then try to complete the quiz. If you have trouble, only then should you look at the relevant part of the text in more details.

You should not wait until you have completed the homework before taking the quizzes! The quizzes will be on the material we are going to cover that week and so are not meant to test your knowledge of material we have already covered. They are intended to get you thinking a little about the material before coming to lectures.

Each quiz will be graded as follows: you will get 2 points for submitting the quiz. Each question is worth 1 point up to a maximum score of 5 points. There will usually be more than 3 questions so you don't need to get every question right. Please, don't spend too much time on the quiz! Each quiz is worth only half a point for your final grade and you get almost half that just by completing the quiz. The worst case scenario is if you get every question wrong on every quiz and then you will only lose 3 points on your final grade! However, if you spend hours on the quiz, you won't have as much time to do the homework which contributes a greater percentage of your final grade.

Quiz 02 (Sections 2.3-2.6)

  1. In example 1 pp. 52-53, if \(Q(0)\) is not \(25\), is there a \(t\) such that \(Q(t)=25\)?

  2. Is the following equation linear? \[ ty' + 2y = 4 sin (t) \] T
  3. Let \(y_1\) and \(y_2\) be solutions of \(y' + p(t)y = g(t)\). Is \(y_1 + y_2\) also a solution?

    T

  4. Let \(y_1\) and y2 be solutions of \(y' + p(t)y = g(t)\) with \(y_1(0)\) not equal to \(y_2(0)\). Can the curves \(y_1(t)\), \(y_2(t)\) cross? That is does there exist a \(t\) with \(y_1(t)\) = \(y_2(t)\)?

    F

    F

Quiz 03 (Sections 3.1-3.3)

  1. Suppose \(ar^2 + br + c\) has real, positive distinct roots and let \(y(t)\) be the solution of \[ \begin{cases} ay'' + by' + c &= 0 \\ y(0) &= 1 \\ y'(0) &= 1. \end{cases} \] Which of the following is true?
    1. \(y \to \infty\) as \(t\to\infty\).
    2. \(y \to -\infty\) as \(t\to\infty\).
    3. \(y \to 0\) as \(t\to\infty\).

    a

  2. Suppose \(r_0=0\) is a root of \(ar^2 + br + c\). Does \(ay'' + by' + c = 0\) admit a constant function as solution?

    T

  3. Let \(y_1\), \(y_2\) be solutions of \(ay'' + by' + cy = 0\) such that the Wronskian \(W(y_1, y_2) (t_0) = 0\).

    True or false? \(W(y_1,y_2)(t) = 0\) for all \(t\).

    T

  4. How many fundamental sets of solutions does a second order linear homogeneous equation have?
    1. 0
    2. 1
    3. \(\infty\)

    c

  5. Suppose the characteristic equation \(ar^2 + br + c = 0\) has one complex root. Is the other root
    1. real,
    2. zero or
    3. complex?

    c

  6. If the characteristic equation \(ar^2 + br + c = 0\) has complex roots, does the equation \(ay'' + by' + c = 0\) admit real solutions?

    T

  1. Why isn't \(C_1 e^{r t} + C_2 e^{rt}\) the general form of a linear, constant coefficient, homogeneous equation where the characteristic equation has the repeated root \(r\)?

    2. There are infinitely many \(C_1, C_2\) satisfying the initial conditions.
    3. There exists \(y_0, y_0'\) where no \(C_1, C_2\) can be chosen to satisfy the initial conditions.

  2. How many equations does the method of Variations of Parameters produce for \(u_1', u_2'\)?
    1. 0
    2. 1
    3. 2

    c

  3. Let \(y_1, y_2\) be a fundamental set of solutions for the homogeneous, linear equation \(y'' + p(t)y' + q(t)y = 0\) and \(Y(t)\) be a solution of the non-homogeneous, linear equation \(y'' + p(t)y' + q(t)y = g(t)\). Which of the following is the general form of the solution to the non-homogeneous equation?
    1. Y(t)
    2. C1 y1 + C2 y2 + Y
    3. C1 y1 + C2 y2

    b

  4. What is a particular solution for the equation \(y'' - 3y' - 4y = 2\sin t\)?

Quiz 06

  1. Which of the following is the first order system corresponding to the equation \(u'' + 1/2 u' + 2u = 0\)?
    1. \[ \begin{split} x_1 &= u \\ x_2 &= -1/2 x_2 - 2x_1 \end{split} \]
    2. \[ \begin{split} x_1' &= u \\ x_2' &= -1/2 x_1 - 2x_1 \end{split} \]
    3. \[ \begin{split} x_1' &= u \\ x_2' &= -1/2 x_2 - 2x_1 \end{split} \]

    c

  2. If \(\det A \ne 0\), what is \(A^{-1}\)?
    1. \(\frac{M_{ij}}{\det A}\)
    2. \(\frac{C_{ji}}{\det A}\)
    3. \(\frac{(-1)^{i+j} C_{ij}}{\det A}\)

    b

  3. What are the eigenvalues of \[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -1 \\ 4 & -2 \end{pmatrix} \]
    1. \(2\), \(-1\)
    2. \(-2\), \(1\)
    3. \(2\), \(1\)

    a

  4. Are the vectors \[ \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}, \begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} \]
    1. linearly dependent, or
    2. linearly independent?

    b

  5. My apologies, this question is poorly worded. The Wronksian is certainly non-zero at \(t_1\), but the vectors \(\{x_1(t_1), \cdots, x_n(t_1)\}\) could be linearly independent or linearly dependent at \(t_1\). Thus the most correct is "neither". If \(n\) solutions \(x_1, \dotsc, x_n\) to a linear first order system of \(n\) equations in \(n\) unknowns are linearly independent at \(t_0\), are they
    1. linearly independent at \(t_1\ne t_0\),
    2. linearly dependent at \(t_1\ne t_0\), or
    3. neither?

    c

  6. What is the maximum number of functions possible for a fundamental set of solutions to a system of \(n\) equations?
    1. 1
    2. \(n\)
    3. 0
    4. \(\infty\)

    b
