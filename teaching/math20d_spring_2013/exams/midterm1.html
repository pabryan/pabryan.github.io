MATH20D Midterm 1

Instructions

  1. You may use one page of notes (written on both sides), but no books or other assistance during this exam.
  2. Write your Name, PID, and Section on the front of your Blue Book.
  3. Write your solutions clearly in your Blue Book
    1. Carefully indicate the number and letter of each question and question part.
    2. Present your answers in the same order they appear in the exam.
    3. Start each question on a new page.
  4. Read each question carefully, and answer each question completely.
  5. Show all of your work; no credit will be given for unsupported answers.
  6. The long questions require full proofs, the short answer questions only require brief justification.

Long Questions

These questions require full proofs.

  1. (5 points) Find the solution of the initial value problem \[ \begin{cases} y' &= t^2 (y - 1) \\ y(1) &= y_0 \end{cases} \] for any \(y_0\in \RR\).
  2. (5 points) Consider the autonomous equations \[ y' = y^2 - y \]
    1. Find the equilibrium solutions.
    2. Determine which equilibrium solutions are stable and which are not.
  3. (5 points)
    1. Find a fundamental set of solutions for the initial value problem \[ \begin{cases} y'' + y' - 6y &= 0 \\ y(0) &= y_0 \\ y'(0) &= y_0'. \end{cases} \] Make sure you prove that you have found a fundamental set of solutions.
    2. What is the solution when \(y_0=1\), \(y_0' = -3\)?

Short Answer

These questions only require brief justification.

  1. (1 point) Let \(y_1\) and \(y_2\) be solutions of \(y' + p(t)y = g(t)\) with \(g(t)\ne 0\). Is \(y_1 + y_2\) also a solution?
  2. (1 point) Let \(y_1\) and y2 be solutions of \(y' + p(t)y = g(t)\) with \(y_1(0)\) not equal to \(y_2(0)\). Can the curves \(y_1(t)\), \(y_2(t)\) cross? That is does there exist a \(t\) with \(y_1(t)\) = \(y_2(t)\)?
  3. (1 point) Given a solution \(y\) to the logistic growth equation \(y' = r(1 - y/K)y\), if for some \(t_1\) we have \(y(t_1)>K\), does there exist a \(t_2\) with \(y(t_2)<K\)?
  4. (1 point) Suppose \(ar^2 + br + c\) has real, positive distinct roots \(r_1, r_2\) and let \(y(t)\) be the solution of \[ \begin{cases} ay'' + by' + c &= 0 \\ y(0) &= 1 \\ y'(0) &= r_1. \end{cases} \] Which of the following is true?
    1. \(y \to \infty\) as \(t\to\infty\).
    2. \(y \to -\infty\) as \(t\to\infty\).
    3. \(y \to 0\) as \(t\to\infty\).
  5. (1 point) Let \(y_1\), \(y_2\) be solutions of \(ay'' + by' + cy = 0\) such that the Wronskian \(W(y_1, y_2) (t_1) \ne 0\). Is there a solution of the form \(y = C_1 y_1 + C_2 y_2\) to the initial value problem \[ \begin{cases} ay'' + by' + cy &= 0 \\ y(t_0) &= y_0 \\ y'(t_0) &= y_0' \end{cases} \] for \(t_0 \ne t_1\)?
