Math20C Final

Instructions

  1. You may use two pages of notes (written on both sides), but no books or other assistance during this exam.
  2. Write your Name, PID, and Section on the front of your Blue Book.
  3. Write your solutions clearly in your Blue Book
    1. Carefully indicate the number and letter of each question and question part.
    2. Present your answers in the same order they appear in the exam.
    3. Start each question on a new page.
  4. Read each question carefully, and answer each question completely.
  5. Show all of your work; no credit will be given for unsupported answers.
  6. The long questions require full proofs, the short answer questions only require brief justification.

Long Questions

These questions require full proofs.

  1. (10 points)
    1. Find a vector equation for the line \(\mathcal{L}\) passing through the points \(P=(2, 1, 0)\) and \(Q=(1, 7, 1)\).
    2. Show that for any real numbers \(a\) and \(b\), the vector \(\vec{u} = \langle 6a + b, a, b\rangle\) is perpindicular to the line \(\mathcal{L}\).
    3. Find the normal vector to the plane passing through \(P\), containing the line \(\mathcal{L}\) and the vector \(\vec{u} = \langle 6, 1, 0\rangle\) based at \(P\).
  2. (10 points) Consider the function \[ f(x, y) = x^2y - y + x - 2 \]
    1. Find the critical points of \(f\).
    2. Classify the critical points as either local maxima, local minima or saddle points.
    3. What are the global maximum and minimum values of \(f\) over the unit square \(S = \{(x,y) : 0\leq x \leq 1, 0 \leq y \leq 1\}\)?
  3. (10 points). Calculate the area of the disk \(D = \{(x,y): x^2 + y^2 \leq R^2\}\) of radius \(R\) in the plane using the following two methods:
    1. Write the upper hemisphere \(D^+ = \{(x,y) \in D: y\geq 0\}\) as the region bounded between the \(x\) axis and the graph of a function \(y = f(x)\). Hint: Use the equation defining the boundary of \(D\) to solve for \(y\) in terms of \(x\). Argue by symmetry that the area of \(D\) is twice the area of \(D^+\) and then evaluate the integral of \(1\) over \(D^+\) to obtain the area. Hint: You may find a trigometric substituion useful in evaluating the \(x\) integral.
    2. Use polar coordinates \((x, y) = r(\cos\theta, \sin \theta)\) and evaluate the integral of \(1\) over the domain \(0\leq r \leq R\), \(0\leq \theta \leq 2\pi\).
  4. (10 points) The electric potential \(\Phi\) of a point charge located at the origin is (up to a multiplicative constant) \[ \Phi(x, y) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}}. \] Suppose we have a magnet located at the origin and a metal bead on a ellipitical loop of string centered on the origin. We can model this situation mathematically by considering the magnet as a point charge at the origin and the bead as a point on the ellipse \[ \left(\frac{x}{a}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{y}{b}\right)^2 = 1. \]
    1. Using Lagrange multipliers, find the points of minimum and maximum potential experienced by the metal bead.

      Hint: You can simplify \(\grad \Phi\) since you only need to know the direction and not the magnitude.

    2. Let \(a=1\), \(b=2\). Determine the maximum and minimim values of \(\Phi\) over the ellipse.
    3. Bonus (5 points). There is one situation where the Lagrange equation is satisfied at all points \((x,y)\) on the ellipse. Can you find it?
  5. (10 points) Let \(f(x,y) = x e^{x-y} - yx^2\).
    1. Find the equation of the plane tangent to \(z = f (x, y)\) at the point \((2, 2, -6)\).
    2. Find the directional derivative of \(f\) at the point \((2, 2)\) in the direction \(\langle 1, 4 \rangle\).
    3. In what direction is the maximum rate of change of \(f\) at the point \((2, 2)\)?
  6. (10 points)
    1. Draw the contour lines in the \(xy\)-plane, of the surface \((x - z + 2)^2 + y^2 = 4\) for the values \(z = 0, 1, 2\). Note that this surface is not the graph of a function \(z = f(x,y)\) so the contour lines may intersect.
    2. Find a parametrization of the intersection of the plane \(x = 2\) with the surface \[ (x - z + 2)^2 + y^2 = 4. \]
  7. (10 points) Consider the tetrahedron \(T\) shown in the image.

./tetra.pdf

\(T\) is the region bounded by the planes \(x=0\), \(y=0\), \(z=0\), and the plane \(\mathcal{P}\) containing the points \((1, 0, 0)\), \((0,2,0)\) and \((0,0,1)\).

  1. Show that the equation of the plane \(\mathcal{P}\) is \[ x + \frac{1}{2}y + z = 1. \]
  2. Evaluate \[ \int\int\int_T xyz dV. \]

Short Answer

These questions only require brief justification. No points will be awarded for solutions without any justification. Each question is worth 1 point.

  1. When is the cross prouct \(\mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{w}\) equal to zero?
    1. When \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\) are orthogonal.
    2. When \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\) are linearly independent.
    3. When \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\) are parallel.
  2. Suppose that a plane \(\mathcal{P}\) with normal vector \(\mathbf{n}\) and a line \(\mathcal{L}\) with direction vector \(\mathbf{v}\) both pass through the origin and that \(\mathbf{n} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0\). Which of the following statements is correct?
    1. \(\mathcal{L}\) is contained in \(\mathcal{P}\).
    2. \(\mathcal{L}\) is orthogonal to \(\mathcal{P}\).
  3. Which of the following does not parametrise a line?
    1. \(\mathbf{r}_1(t) = \langle 8-t, 2t, 3t\rangle\)
    2. \(\mathbf{r}_2(t) = t^3\mathbf{i} - 7t^3\mathbf{j} + t^3\mathbf{k}\)
    3. \(\mathbf{r}_3(t) = \langle 8-4t^3, 2 + 5t^2, 9t^3\rangle\)
  4. What is \(f_x\) where \(f(x,y,z) = (\sin yz) e^{z^3-z^{-1}\sqrt{y}}\)?
    1. \(0\).
    2. \(\infty\).
    3. Not defined.
  5. Assuming the hypotheses of Clairaut's Theorem are satisfied (i.e. all third partial derivatives are continuous), which of the following partial derivatives are equal to \(f_{xxy}\)?
    1. \(f_{xyx}\)
    2. \(f_{yyx}\)
    3. \(f_{xyy}\)
  6. You are standing at a point where the temperature gradient vector is pointing in the northeast (NE) direction. In which direction(s) should you walk to avoid a change in temperature (choose all correct answers)?
    1. NE
    2. NW
    3. SE
    4. SW
  7. According to the Chain Rule, \(\partial f/\partial r\) is equal to (choose the correct answer):
    1. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial r} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial s}\)
    2. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial r} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \frac{\partial y}{\partial r}\)
    3. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial r} \frac{\partial r}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial s} \frac{\partial s}{\partial x}\)
  8. Let \(f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2\) and \(g(x,y) = x^2-y^2\). Notice that \(P=(0,0)\) is a critical point of both \(f\) and \(g\). Which of the following is correct?
    1. \(P\) is a local minimum of both \(f\) and \(g\).
    2. \(P\) is a local minimum of \(f\) and a saddle point of \(g\).
    3. \(P\) is a local minimum of \(g\) and a saddle point of \(f\).
    4. \(P\) is a saddle point of both \(f\) and \(g\).
  9. Suppose that the point \(P\) is such that \(g(P)=0\) and \(\nabla g (P) \ne 0\). Suppose further that the maximum value of \(f(Q)\) among all points \(Q\) with \(g(Q)=0\) occurs at the point \(P\). Which of the following is true?
    1. \(\nabla f\) is tangent at \(P\) to the curve \(g(Q)=0\).
    2. \(\nabla f\) is orthogonal at \(P\) to the curve \(g(Q)=0\).
  10. Let \(D\) be the unit disk, i.e. the circle of radius one with centre the origin. If the maximum value of \(f(x,y)\) on \(D\) is \(4\), then the largest possible value of \(\int\int_{D} f(x,y) dA\) is (choose the correct answer):
    1. \(4\)
    2. \(4\pi\)
    3. \(\frac{4}{\pi}\)
