Math20C Midterm 2

Instructions

  1. You may use one pages of notes (written on both sides), but no books or other assistance during this exam.
  2. Write your Name, PID, and Section on the front of your Blue Book.
  3. Write your solutions clearly in your Blue Book
    1. Carefully indicate the number and letter of each question and question part.
    2. Present your answers in the same order they appear in the exam.
    3. Start each question on a new page.
  4. Read each question carefully, and answer each question completely.
  5. Show all of your work; no credit will be given for unsupported answers.
  6. The long questions require full proofs, the short answer questions only require brief justification.

Long Questions

These questions require full proofs.

  1. (10 points) Let \(f(x,y) = x^2 - y^2\).
    1. Find a parametrisation for the level curves of \(f\). Hint: Solve \(f(x,y) = C\) for \(x\) in terms of \(y\).
    2. Find the gradient of \(f\).
    3. Show explicitly that the gradient of \(f\) is orthogonal to the level curves of \(f\). That is, \(\nabla f\) is orthogonal to \(\vec{v}\) where \(\vec{v}\) is the tangent vector to the level curve.
  2. (10 points) Find the area of the largest inscribed rectangle \(R\) in the ellispe \(\tfrac{x^2}{a^2} + \tfrac{y^2}{b^2} = 1\). Recall an inscribed rectangle is one contained within the ellipse and with corners lying on the ellipse. Hint: Let \(f(x,y)\) be the area of \(R\) where \((x,y)\) is say the upper right hand corner of \(R\) (draw the picture!) and optimize \(f\) subject to the constraint that \((x,y)\) lie on the ellipse.
  3. (10 points) This question describes an alternative proof of the fact that a function changes fastest in the direction of it's gradient. We can parametrise the set of unit vectors (those with length \(1\)) in the plane by \(\langle \cos \theta, \sin \theta \rangle\), \(\theta \in [0, 2\pi]\). That is, every such vector is a unit vector and every unit vector is of this form. Denote the directional derivative of a differentiable function \(f\) in the direction \(\langle \cos \theta, \sin \theta \rangle\) by \(D_{\theta} f\).
    1. Show that \[ (D_{\theta} f) (a, b) = \pd[f]{x} (a,b) \cos \theta + \pd[f]{y} (a, b) \sin \theta. \]
    2. Fixing \(a\) and \(b\) and thinking of \((D_{\theta} f)(a,b)\) as a function of \(\theta\), show that \[ \pd{\theta} (D_{\theta} f) (a,b) = -\pd[f]{x} (a,b) \sin \theta + \pd[f]{y}(a,b) \cos \theta. \]
    3. Prove that \((D_{\theta} f) (a,b)\) has a critical point at values of \(\theta\) where \(\langle \cos \theta, \sin \theta \rangle\) is parallel with \(\nabla f\).
    4. Show that \((D_{\theta} f) (a,b)\) is maximum when \(\langle \cos \theta, \sin \theta \rangle\) and \(\nabla f\) point in the same direction, and minimum when \langle \(\cos \theta, \sin \theta \rangle\) and \(\nabla f\) point in opposite directions. Hint: Apply the second derivative test for functions of one variable.

Short Answer

These questions only require brief justification. No points will be given for answers without justification.

  1. (1 point) Let \(f=f(x,y)\), \(x=x(r, s)\) and \(y=y(r,s)\) be differentiable functions. According to the Chain Rule, \(\partial f/\partial r\) is equal to (choose the correct answer):
    1. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial r} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial s}\)
    2. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial r} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \frac{\partial y}{\partial r}\)
    3. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial r} \frac{\partial r}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial s} \frac{\partial s}{\partial x}\)
  2. (1 point) How will the contour maps of \[ f(x, y) = x \quad \text{and} \quad g(x, y) = 2x \] with contour interval \(1\) look different?
    1. The level curves of \(f\) are closer.
    2. They won't look different. They are the same.
    3. The level curves of \(g\) are closer.
  3. (1 point) Suppose that \(f(x,0) = 3\) for all \(x\ne 0\) and \(f(0, y) = 5\) for all \(y\ne 0\). What can you conclude about \(\lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)} f(x,y)\)?
    1. It is equal to \(3\).
    2. It is equal to \(5\).
    3. It is equal to \(\infty\).
    4. It is not defined.
  4. (1 point) Assume that \(f(2,3)=8\), \(f_x(2,3)=5\), \(f_y(2,3)=7\). Which of the following is the linearization of \(f\) at \((2,3)\)?
    1. \(L(x,y) = 8 + 5x + 7y\)
    2. \(L(x,y) = 8 + 5(x-2) + 7(y-3)\)
  5. (1 point) Let \(f\) be continuous on a domain \(D\). Which of the following is true?
    1. If \(D\) is closed and bounded, then \(f\) has a maximum on \(D\).
    2. If \(D\) is neither closed nor bounded then \(f\) has no maximum on \(D\).
    3. If \(D=\{(x,y): 0\leq x \leq 1, 0\leq y \leq 1\}\), then \(f\) need not have a maximum.
    4. If \(D=\{(x,y): x>0, y>0\}\) then \(f\) takes on neither a maximum nor a minimum.
