Quizzes

Instructions

There will be an online quiz due each Monday at 11am. The quiz is available on TED and can be taken anytime before the due date. You don't need to complete it in one sitting.

You should read the relevant sections (see Schedule below) of the text in advance of taking the quiz. Just skim through the text, reading the definitions and theorem statements (not necessarily proofs) and skim over a few of the examples. Then try to complete the quiz. If you have trouble, only then should you look at the relevant part of the text in more details.

You should not wait until you have completed the homework before taking the quizzes! The quizzes will be on the material we are going to cover that week and so are not meant to test your knowledge of material we have already covered. They are intended to get you thinking a little about the material before coming to lectures.

Each quiz will be graded as follows: you will get 2 points for submitting the quiz. Each question is worth 1 point up to a maximum score of 5 points. There will usually be more than 3 questions so you don't need to get every question right. Please, don't spend too much time on the quiz! Each quiz is worth only half a point for your final grade and you get almost half that just by completing the quiz. The worst case scenario is if you get every question wrong on every quiz and then you will only lose 3 points on your final grade! However, if you spend hours on the quiz, you won't have as much time to do the homework which contributes a greater percentage of your final grade.

Quiz 02

  1. 12.3.5 Let \(\mathbf{u}_{\parallel}\) be the projection of \(\mathbf{u}\) along \(\mathbf{v}\). Which of the following is the projection of \(\mathbf{u}\) along the vector \(2\mathbf{v}\)?

    3. \(2 \mathbf{u}_{\parallel}\)

  2. 12.4.2 The angle between two unit vectors \(\mathbf{e}\) and \(\mathbf{f}\) is \(\tfrac{\pi}{6}\). What is the length of \(\mathbf{e} \times \mathbf{f}\)?

    0.5

  3. 12.4.6 When is the cross prouct \(\mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{w}\) equal to zero?
    1. When \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\) are orthogonal.

    3. When \(\mathbf{v}\) and \(\mathbf{w}\) are parallel.

    c

  4. 12.5.2 The vector \(\mathbf{k}\) is normal to which of the following planes?

Quiz 03

  1. (13.1.1) Which of the following does not parametrise a line?
    1. \(\mathbf{r}_1(t) = \langle 8-t, 2t, 3t\rangle\)
    2. \(\mathbf{r}_2(t) = t^3\mathbf{i} - 7t^3\mathbf{j} + t^3\mathbf{k}\)
    3. \(\mathbf{r}_3(t) = \langle 8-4t^3, 2 + 5t^2, 9t^3\rangle\)

    c

  2. (13.1.3) Which projection of \(\langle \cos t, \cos 2t, \sin t\rangle\) is a circle?
    1. projection onto the \(xy\)-plane.
    2. projection onto the \(xz\)-plane.
    3. projection onto the \(yz\)-plane.

    b

  3. (13.2.2) True or false? The derivative of a vector-valued function is defined as the limit of the difference quotient, just as in the scalar-valued case.

    True.

  4. (13.2.5) True or false? The derivative of a vector-valued function is the slope of the tangent line, just as in the scalar-valued case.

    False.

  5. (13.3.2) Two cars travel in the same direction along the same roller coaster (at different times). Which of the following statements about their velocity vectors at a given point \(P\) on the roller coaster is/are true?
    1. The velocity vectors are identical.
    2. The velocity vectors point in the same direction but may have different lengths.
    3. The velocity vectors may point in opposite directions.

    b

  6. (13.3.4) What is the length of the path traced by \(\mathbf{r}(t)\) for \(4 \leq t \leq 10\) if \(\mathbf{r}(t)\) is an arc-length parametrisation?

    6

Quiz 05

  1. (14.1.1) What is the difference between a horizontal trace and a level curve?
    1. Nothing. They are the same.
    2. The horizontal trace is the graph of \(f\) over the level curve.
    3. The level curve is a graph of \(f\) over the horizontal trace.

    b

  2. (14.1.5) How will the contour maps of \[ f(x, y) = x \quad \text{and} \quad g(x, y) = 2x \] with contour interval \(1\) look different?
    1. The level curves of \(f\) are closer.
    2. They won't look different. They are the same.
    3. The level curves of \(g\) are closer.

    c

  3. (14.2.2) Suppose that \(f(x, y)\) is continuous at \((2,3)\) and that \(f(2, y) = y^3\) for \(y\ne 3\). What is the value of \(f(2, 3)\)?

    27

  4. (14.2.4) Suppose that \(f(x,0) = 3\) for all \(x\ne 0\) and \(f(0, y) = 5\) for all \(y\ne 0\). What can you conclude about \(\lim_{(x,y)\to(0,0)} f(x,y)\)?
    1. It is equal to \(0\).
    2. It is equal to \(\infty\).
    3. It is not defined.

    a

  5. (14.3.4) What is \(f_x\) where \(f(x,y,z) = (\sin yz) e^{z^3-z^{-1}\sqrt{y}}\)?
    1. \(0\).
    2. \(\infty\).
    3. Not defined.

    a

  6. (14.3.5) Assuming the hypotheses of Clairaut's Theorem are satisfied (i.e. all third partial derivatives are continuous), which of the following partial derivatives are equal to \(f_{xxy}\)?
    1. \(f_{xyx}\)
    2. \(f_{yyx}\)
    3. \(f_{xyy}\)

    a

Quiz 06

  1. (14.4.1) How is the linearization of \(f(x,y)\) at \((a,b)\) defined?
    1. \(L(x,y) = f(a,b) + f_x(a,b)(x-a) + f_y(a,b)(y-b)\)
    2. \(L(x,y) = f(a,b) + f_x(a,b) + f_y(a,b)\)
    3. \(L(x,y) = f(a,b) + f_x(a,b)(x-a)\)

    a

  2. (14.4.3) Assume that \(f(2,3)=8\), \(f_x(2,3)=5\), \(f_y(2,3)=7\). Which of the following is the linearization of \(f\) at \((2,3)\)?
    1. \(L(x,y) = 8 + 5x + 7y\)
    2. \(L(x,y) = 8 + 5(x-2) + 7(y-3)\)

    b

  3. (14.5.2) True or false? A differentiable function increases at the rate \(\|\nabla f_P\|\) in the direction of \(\nabla f_P\)?

    True

  4. (14.5.4) You are standing at a point where the temperature gradient vector is pointing in the northeast (NE) direction. In which direction(s) should you walk to avoid a change in temperature?
    1. NE
    2. NW
    3. SE
    4. SW

    b,c.

  5. (14.6.4) According to the Chain Rule, \(\partial f/\partial r\) is equal to (choose the correct answer):
    1. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial r} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial s}\)
    2. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \frac{\partial x}{\partial r} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \frac{\partial y}{\partial r}\)
    3. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial r} \frac{\partial r}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial s} \frac{\partial s}{\partial x}\)

    b

  6. (14.6.5) Suppose that \(x,y,z\) are functions of the independent variables \(u,v,w\). Which of the following terms appear in the Chain Rule expression for \(\partial f/\partial w\)?
    1. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial v} \frac{\partial x}{\partial v}\)
    2. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial w} \frac{\partial w}{\partial x}\)
    3. \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial z} \frac{\partial z}{\partial w}\)

    c

Quiz 07

  1. (14.7.1) Let \(f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2\) and \(g(x,y) = x^2-y^2\). Notice that \(P=(0,0)\) is a critical point of both \(f\) and \(g\). Which of the following is correct?
    1. \(P\) is a local minimum of both \(f\) and \(g\).
    2. \(P\) is a local minimum of \(f\) and a saddle point of \(g\).
    3. \(P\) is a local minimum of \(g\) and a saddle point of \(f\).
    4. \(P\) is a saddle point of both \(f\) and \(g\).

    b

  2. (14.7.3) Let \(f\) be continuous on a domain \(D\). Which of the following is true?
    1. If \(D\) is closed and bounded, then \(f\) has a maximum on \(D\).
    2. If \(D\) is neither closed nor bounded then \(f\) has no maximum on \(D\).
    3. If \(D=\{(x,y): 0\leq x \leq 1, 0\leq y \leq 1\}\), then \(f\) need not have a maximum.
    4. If \(D=\{(x,y): x>0, y>0\}\) then \(f\) takes on neither a maximum nor a minimum.

    a

  3. (14.8.1) Suppose that the point \(P\) is such that \(G(P)=0\) and \(f(P)\) is maximum for all points \(Q\) with \(g(Q)=0\) and such that \(\nabla g (P) \ne 0\). Which is true?
    1. \(\nabla f\) is tangent to the curve \(g(Q)=0\).
    2. \(\nabla f\) is orthogonal to the curve \(g(Q)=0\).

    b
