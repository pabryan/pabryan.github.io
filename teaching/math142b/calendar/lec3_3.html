Lecture 3.3 Overview

  • Estimating the Remainder

We established a formula for the remainder of the Taylor expansion for \(1/x\). We can now use Taylor Polynomials to approximate \(1/x\) to any desired accuracy, but choosing \(n\) large enough so that the remainder is small. We will discuss how to find \(n\).

  • Taylor Series for Log

We can use our expansion for \(1/x\), which actually amounts to the Geometric Sum Formula, to derive a Taylor expansion for \(\ln x\).

  • Infitely differentiable, non-analytic function

It may help to understand how the local behaviour of a function may be deduced from the Taylor polynomials, by considering a situation where things go wrong!
